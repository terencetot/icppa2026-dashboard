Selection Intelligence  Bias-corrected scores for fair comparison across reviewers

📚 Methodology : Selection Intelligence

Adjusted Score (Bias-corrected): Each raw score is corrected for the reviewer's individual bias: Adjusted score = mean(raw_scorei − biasi), where biasi = reviewer mean − global mean across all reviewers. This neutralises differences in reviewer strictness or leniency.

Adjusted Z-Score: Measures how far each abstract stands from the adjusted-score mean: Z = (adjusted score − μ) / σ. Z ≥ 1.5 indicates a statistically strong abstract; Z ≤ −1.5 indicates a weak one.

Confidence Level: Based on the number of reviews received: ●●● = 3 reviews (reliable), ●●○ = 2 (moderate), ●○○ = 1 (use with caution).

Recommendation: Derived from the adjusted Z-score: Strong Accept if Z ≥ 1.5 • Accept if Z ≥ 0.5 • Review if Z ≥ −0.5 • Weak otherwise.

★ The top 30 are highlighted as an indicative selection: 10 oral + 20 poster. Each group is allocated using the Hamilton (Largest-Remainder) method on two axes simultaneously: (1) Disease domain — each domain receives slots proportional to its share of submissions, preventing over-representation; (2) Official language — Anglophone, Francophone, Lusophone and Other groups each receive proportional slots; Lusophone receives ≥1 slot if its best abstract is statistically above the global mean (quality gate: adj. score ≥ mean). Within each cell the highest-scoring abstract by adjusted score is selected. Use Adjusted Score and Confidence Level for the final decision.
